name: Report conciseness
description: User's professor is domain-expert; reports should skip basics and focus on what's new/proposed
type: feedback
---

When writing reports or documents for the user's professor, skip introductory explanations of standard concepts (OU process, cumulants, etc.) — the audience already knows them. Focus on the novel contribution, proposed changes, and any bugs/issues found.

**Why:** User explicitly asked to shorten a report that included standard OU definitions, saying "my professor knows what the OU process and cumulants are."

**How to apply:** In any technical writeup, assume the reader has graduate-level familiarity with stochastic processes and statistics. Lead with the new idea, not the background.
